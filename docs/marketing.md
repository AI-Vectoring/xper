## Headline

**Your AI knows what it needs to know. Nothing more.**

---

## The Problem

Every AI assistant has the same dirty secret: it works by guessing what context might be relevant, pulling chunks of text based on keyword and similarity matching, and hoping the right information ends up in the prompt.

When it works, it looks like magic. When it does not, your AI confidently answers a question with the wrong information from the wrong part of your docs — and your users trust it anyway.

The root cause is not the AI. It is how the knowledge is delivered to it.

---

## The Solution

xper structures your knowledge the way a good expert thinks: hierarchically, with clear paths from general to specific.

When a user asks a question, xper does not dump chunks into a prompt. It navigates. It reads the top of the tree, reasons about which branch leads toward the answer, follows that branch, and keeps going until it reaches exactly the right node. Then it answers from there — and only from there.

The result is an AI that gives precise, grounded, auditable answers. You can see exactly which nodes it visited to reach every answer. No black box.

---

## Key Differentiators

**Navigational, not retrieval-based.**
xper reasons about the structure of your knowledge. It does not guess by similarity. The same question takes the same path every time.

**Built and maintained by AI.**
Your team does not hand-author the knowledge tree. Point xper at your existing documentation — a URL, a PDF, a GitHub repo — and an LLM structures it automatically. When your docs change, re-run the pipeline.

**Auditable by design.**
Every answer comes with a traversal path: the exact sequence of nodes the AI visited to reach the answer. When something is wrong, you know exactly where in the tree to fix it.

**Falls back gracefully.**
For questions that span multiple topics or that navigation cannot resolve, xper falls back to semantic search across the full knowledge base. Navigation and search are complementary, not competing.

**No hallucination from outside the knowledge base.**
xper does not synthesize answers from training data. It navigates to the answer or tells you it could not find one.

## Use Cases

**Product documentation**
Turn your existing docs into a precise, navigable knowledge base. Users ask questions; xper finds answers from the right section, not just the nearest chunk.

**Internal knowledge management**
Give your team an AI that knows your processes, policies, and systems — and navigates them the way a senior employee would.

**Compliance and policy**
For regulated industries where the source of an answer matters as much as the answer itself, xper's traversal path provides a documented chain of provenance.

**Technical support**
Reduce tier-1 support load with an AI that gives specific, correct answers — not plausible-sounding guesses — backed by your actual support documentation.

**Onboarding**
New employees and customers navigate complex information at their own pace, with an AI that guides them through the right content in the right order.

## What Sets xper Apart from RAG

Most AI knowledge systems are built on vector search: your documents are broken into chunks, embedded into a high-dimensional space, and retrieved by proximity to the query. It is fast to set up and works reasonably well on flat, unstructured content.

xper is different. It exploits the structure that exists in well-organized knowledge — the same structure that makes a good manual easy for a human expert to navigate. The AI reasons about that structure rather than approximating it with similarity scores.

The practical result: fewer wrong answers, fewer false positives, full auditability, and a knowledge base that gets better as your documentation improves — not just as your embedding model improves.
